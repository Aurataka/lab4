Why visit Tokyo

Tokyo is a city of contrasts, where centuries-old temples sit beside skyscrapers, bustling shopping districts mix with serene parks, and local cuisine delights travelers on every corner. It’s a destination that blends tradition and modernity seamlessly.

From its reliable train system and countless entertainment options to its cultural landmarks and vibrant neighborhoods, Tokyo offers something for every traveler—whether you’re looking for quiet reflection or neon-lit excitement.

Top attractions

  • Sensō-ji Temple
  • Meiji Jingu
  • Shibuya Crossing
  • Tokyo Skytree

Sensō-ji Temple

Tip: Visit early in the morning for fewer crowds and peaceful walks.

Sensō-ji is Tokyo’s oldest temple, located in Asakusa. It features a grand entrance gate, a five-story pagoda, and Nakamise Street lined with shops selling souvenirs and snacks. The temple grounds are perfect for photos and exploring traditional architecture.

Meiji Jingu

Tip: Take time to enjoy the peaceful forest paths leading to the shrine.

Set in a lush forest near Harajuku, Meiji Jingu is one of Tokyo’s most serene spots. Broad pathways lined with tall torii gates lead to the main shrine, offering a calming escape from the city. It’s a must-visit for cultural and historical appreciation.

Shibuya Crossing

Tip: For the best view, watch from a nearby café overlooking the intersection.

Shibuya Crossing is one of the busiest pedestrian intersections in the world. It’s surrounded by shops, restaurants, and nightlife. The area’s energy and bright lights perfectly represent Tokyo’s modern vibe and make it a popular photo spot.

Tokyo Skytree

Tip: Go near sunset to see Tokyo transform into a sea of lights.

Tokyo Skytree is the tallest structure in Japan, featuring observation decks with panoramic city views. On clear days, you can see Mt. Fuji. The base of the tower includes shopping, dining, and an aquarium, making it perfect for a half-day visit.

Good to know

  • Use Suica or PASMO cards for convenient travel and shopping.
  • Cash is still common, but many places accept credit cards.
  • English signs are widely available in train stations and major attractions.
